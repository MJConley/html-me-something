Places I Was Stationed While In the Military

  • Lackland AFB, Texas
  • Osan AB, South Korea
  • Nellis AFB, Nevada
  • Ramstein AB, Germany
  • Lambert IAP, St. Louis, Missouri

Lackland AFB, Texas

I joined the United States Air Force and attended basic training at Lackland AFB.
Lackland AFB is located in southern Texas near the city of San Antonio. I stayed
longer than most new airforce trainees since I was also attending foreign language school.

Osan AB, South Korea

One of my assignments was what the airforce calls a remote assignment. This normally
means that the facilities and medical care are not up to american standards. I was stationed
in a small village about an hour south of the capital Seoul. The assignment is normally for
a time period between 12 and 15 months if you are single. Most remote assignments do not allow
you to take your family if you are married.

Nellis AFB, Nevada

Nellis AFB is located outside Las Vegas, Nevada. I worked at Nellis a few days a week
and worked on the Nellis range the other days. While at work I got the opportunity to
work on a new aircraft, the F117A Stealth Fighter. I spent a lot of time enjoying the
many tourist areas of Las Vegas.

Ramstein AB, Germany

I got the chance to be stationed at Ramstein AB in Germany. I love to travel so being
stationed in Europe was amazing. We lived in a little town about an hour south of
Frankfurt Germany. We were able to travel all around Europe during the three years there.

Lambert IAP, St. Louis, Missouri

One of my last assignments was a special duty assignment at Lambert IAP. The airforce charters
commericial airplanes to move militrary members and their families to overseas destinations.
I worked at commercial airports at both St.Louis and Dallas supporting these missions.
